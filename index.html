Monte Carlo Pi Estimator

Throwing random darts to calculate the value of π

How It Works

Imagine a square dartboard with a circle drawn perfectly inside it. If you throw darts entirely at random, the chance of a dart landing inside the circle is equal to the ratio of the circle's area to the square's area.

Area of Circle = πr²

Area of Square = (2r)² = 4r²

Ratio = πr² / 4r² = π / 4

By keeping track of the total darts thrown and how many landed inside the circle, we can estimate π using this simple formula:

π ≈ 4 × (Inside / Total)
